# ACP Share FAQ (Research + Creator Friendly)

## Q1. Is ACP another agent runtime?
No. ACP is not an execution framework.

## Q2. Is ACP trying to replace MCP or A2A?
No. ACP complements communication and execution layers with accountability semantics.

## Q3. Is ACP a transport protocol?
No. ACP does not define message transport.

## Q4. Is ACP a payment rail?
No. ACP defines settlement intent semantics, not money movement rails.

## Q5. Why does ACP separate verification and settlement?
A proof or verification result should not implicitly execute settlement. The separation keeps dispute and governance controls explicit.

## Q6. What is the minimum concept to remember?
Prompt is not contract. Claim is not evidence. Verification is not settlement.

## Q7. Why should researchers care?
ACP offers a narrow interoperable surface for reproducible accountability analysis across heterogeneous stacks.

## Q8. Why should builders and creators care?
When delegated AI work scales, accountability gaps become operational risk. ACP keeps traceability, acceptance, and dispute boundaries explicit.
