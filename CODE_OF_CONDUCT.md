# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Being respectful and constructive
- Giving and accepting feedback in good faith
- Focusing on the artifact or idea, not the person
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and correcting them promptly

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, threats, or discriminatory language
- Deliberate disruption, trolling, or personal attacks
- Publishing private information without permission
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional public project

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within project spaces and when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Reports may be sent to `CODE_OF_CONDUCT_CONTACT` or the project maintainers listed in `GOVERNANCE.md`.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with discretion.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers should consider the following before taking action:

- Impact of the behavior
- Pattern of the behavior
- Willingness to acknowledge and correct the issue

Possible responses include:

- Private warning
- Temporary suspension from participation
- Permanent removal from project spaces
