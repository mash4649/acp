## 2-sentence launch copy
ACP is an accountability layer for delegated AI work. It is not an execution framework, transport protocol, or payment rail; ACP defines interoperable semantics for agreements, evidence, verification, settlement intent, and dispute/freeze across existing stacks.

## Social post draft (short)
Delegated AI workflows need more than runtime logs.  
ACP introduces an accountability layer with append-only causal history and replayable policy evaluation.  
Not execution. Not transport. Not payments.  
Spec + schemas + vectors are public.

## Social thread draft (3 posts)
1. We keep seeing delegated AI work cross tools, orgs, and runtimes. The missing piece is shared accountability semantics.  
2. ACP defines a narrow boundary: agreement/revision, evidence-bound verification, explicit settlement intent, and dispute/freeze hooks.  
3. ACP does not replace execution frameworks, transport protocols, or payment rails. It complements them with auditable accountability.

## Newsletter blurb
ACP is a public draft for accountability semantics in delegated AI workflows. It separates prompt vs contract, claim vs evidence, and verification vs settlement, while preserving append-only causal history and replayable policy inputs.
